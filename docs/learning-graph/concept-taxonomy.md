# Concept Taxonomy

Categories for organizing the 200 concepts in the "How to Volunteer for Political Canvassing and Door Knocking" learning graph.

## Categories

### 1. Civic Foundations (CIVIC)

Foundational concepts about democracy, elections, and civic participation that provide context for all volunteer activities.

### 2. Campaign Operations (CAMP)

Concepts related to how political campaigns are structured, organized, and run, including strategy, staffing, and voter data.

### 3. Canvassing Fundamentals (CANV)

Core concepts about canvassing as a voter contact method, including turf management, walk lists, apps, and data recording.

### 4. Scripting and Messaging (SCRP)

Concepts related to canvassing scripts, message delivery, campaign messaging, and structured conversation components.

### 5. Volunteering Basics (VOLN)

Concepts about the volunteer experience, including finding opportunities, roles, training, onboarding, and retention.

### 6. First Shift Preparation (FRST)

Concepts specific to preparing for and completing a first volunteer shift, including logistics, anxiety management, and debriefing.

### 7. Door Knocking Skills (DOOR)

Practical skills for door-to-door canvassing, including approach techniques, safety, special environments, and physical considerations.

### 8. Conversation Skills (CONV)

Interpersonal communication skills used in face-to-face voter interactions, including rapport building, listening, and disengagement.

### 9. Phone Banking (PHON)

Concepts related to conducting voter outreach by phone, including technology, scripts, metrics, and virtual operations.

### 10. Text Banking (TEXT)

Concepts related to peer-to-peer texting operations, platforms, compliance, and message management.

### 11. Persuasion Techniques (PERS)

Strategies and methods for persuading undecided voters, including framing, storytelling, and ethical considerations.

### 12. GOTV Operations (GOTV)

Get Out the Vote concepts covering mobilization strategy, Election Day logistics, and last-touch voter contact.

### 13. Deep Canvassing (DEEP)

Specialized concepts about the deep canvassing method, including narrative exchange, empathy-based techniques, and research.

### 14. Evaluation and Capstone (EVAL)

Concepts related to assessing effectiveness, self-improvement, planning, and capstone applied projects.
